### **Part I: Two-Port Networks**

**Lecture 1: Introduction to Two-Port Networks**
*   Definition of One-port vs. Two-port networks.
*   Independent and Dependent variables.
*   **Z-Parameters:** Open Circuit Impedance parameters.
    *   Derivation and equations.
    *   T-equivalent circuit.
*   **Y-Parameters:** Short Circuit Admittance parameters.
    *   Derivation and equations.
    *   Physical realization (Pi-equivalent circuit).
    *   Relationship between Z and Y parameters.

**Lecture 2: Two-Port Networks (Continued)**
*   **Transmission Parameters (ABCD):**
    *   Equations and matrix representation.
    *   Reciprocity condition ($AD - BC = 1$).
*   **Interconnection of Two-Port Networks:**
    *   **Cascaded Interconnection:** Matrix multiplication of Transmission parameters.
    *   **Series Interconnection:** Addition of Z-matrices.
    *   **Parallel Interconnection:** Addition of Y-matrices.

### **Part II: Transient Analysis of DC Circuits**

**Lecture 3: Series Transient Analysis**
*   **Series RL Circuit:**
    *   Current growth and decay equations.
    *   Time constant ($\tau = L/R$).
*   **Series RC Circuit:**
    *   Charging and discharging equations.
    *   Time constant ($\tau = RC$).
*   **Series RLC Circuit:**
    *   Second-order differential equations.
    *   Roots of the characteristic equation ($D_1, D_2$).
    *   **Damping Cases:**
        1.  Overdamped ($\alpha > \omega_0$).
        2.  Critically Damped ($\alpha = \omega_0$).
        3.  Underdamped ($\alpha < \omega_0$).
        4.  Undamped (Oscillatory).

**Lecture 4: Parallel Transient Analysis**
*   **Parallel RL Circuit:**
    *   Current and voltage analysis ($v_L(t)$ and $i(t)$).
*   **Parallel RC Circuit:**
    *   Step response and initial conditions.
*   **Parallel RLC Circuit:**
    *   KCL analysis and differential equations.
    *   Natural frequency ($\omega$) and Damping factor ($\zeta$).
    *   Analysis of roots for Overdamped, Critically Damped, and Underdamped responses.

### **Part III: Laplace Transforms and System Analysis**

**Lecture 5: Laplace Transforms and Transfer Functions**
*   Introduction to Linear Ordinary Differential Equations (First and Second order).
*   **Laplace Transforms:**
    *   Definition and integral formula.
    *   Transforms of standard functions (Step, Exponential, Sin/Cos, Derivatives).
    *   Inverse Laplace Transforms.
*   **Transfer Function:**
    *   Definition ($G(s) = Output/Input$).
    *   Modeling electrical (RC) and mechanical (Mass-Spring-Damper) systems.

**Lecture 6: Network Functions & S-Domain Analysis**
*   Circuit elements in the S-domain ($R, sL, 1/sC$).
*   Driving point Impedance ($Z(s)$) and Admittance ($Y(s)$).
*   **Poles and Zeros:**
    *   Definitions and plotting on the S-plane.
    *   Significance of pole locations for system behavior.
*   **Stability Analysis:**
    *   Stability criterion for active networks.
    *   **Routh-Hurwitz Stability Criterion:** Constructing the Routh array to determine system stability.

### **Part IV: Network Synthesis**

**Lecture 7: Network Synthesis (Part 1)**
*   Introduction to Foster Forms.
*   **RC Network Synthesis:**
    *   Properties of RC Impedance functions.
    *   **Foster Form I:** Partial fraction expansion of $Z_{RC}(s)$.
    *   **Foster Form II:** Partial fraction expansion of $Y_{RC}(s)$.
*   **RL Network Synthesis:**
    *   Properties of RL Impedance functions.
    *   Foster Form I & II for RL networks.
*   **LC Network Synthesis:**
    *   Foster Form I & II for LC networks.

**Lecture 8: Network Synthesis (Part 2)**
*   Synthesis of One-Port RC and RL networks (Review and Examples).
*   Critical frequencies (poles and zeros) placement for RC vs. RL networks.
*   Comparison of $Z(s)$ and $Y(s)$ behavior at $s=0$ and $s=\infty$.

### **Part V: Graph Theory**

**Lecture 9: Graph Theory and Network Equations**
*   **Definitions:** Linear graph, Nodes, Branches.
*   **Graph Properties:**
    *   Subgraph, Connected Graph.
    *   **Tree:** Twigs and Links (Co-tree).
*   **Matrix Representations:**
    *   **Incidence Matrix:** Relationship between nodes and branches.
    *   **Loop Matrix:** Fundamental loops based on links.
    *   **Cut-Set Matrix:** Fundamental cut-sets based on twigs.
    *   KCL and KVL formulation using graph matrices.
